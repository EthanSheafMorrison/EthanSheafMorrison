OVERVIEW

Google Warming is a critical design and research project that investigates how climate change is represented in digital platforms, particularly through Google Earth. Led by Primary Researcher Leon Gurevitch, the project explores the aesthetics and biases embedded in satellite imagery, and how these influence public perception of ecological destruction and resource extraction.

The project uses collected imagery of environmentally disrupted sites—such as mines, oil fields, and industrial wastelands—to examine the visual language of planetary damage. It interrogates how platforms like Google Earth mediate environmental knowledge and what is made visible or hidden within these algorithmically curated interfaces.

APPROACH

We are currently developing an interactive Unreal Engine tool that visualises geolocated environmental data in real time. This system enables viewers to explore layered satellite images and connect them to extractive processes and climate impacts globally.

The tool prioritises user agency, offering intuitive navigation through a database of global extraction sites, allowing users to spatially investigate destruction patterns at varying scales. In doing so, it transforms passive observation into active inquiry, inviting critical engagement with planetary systems.

PROCESS

Initial research phase collecting and categorizing satellite imagery of environmental damage sites globally.

Development of a taxonomy for categorizing different types of extractive and industrial impacts.

Design of interactive visualization systems to make these patterns legible and navigable.

Integration of real-time data feeds to create dynamic representations of environmental change.

"The way we visualize environmental change shapes how we understand it. Google Warming questions the aesthetics of environmental representation in digital platforms." — Leon Gurevitch, Lead Researcher

Visualization Approach

Google Warming employs innovative visualization techniques to make visible the often unseen or ignored aspects of environmental change. Our approach combines multiple data layers with interactive navigation to create a more complete picture of how extraction and industry impact our planet.

Interactive Layers

Our visualization system allows users to toggle between different data layers, combining satellite imagery with climate data, extraction records, and historical imagery. This layered approach reveals patterns and connections that might otherwise remain invisible.

By making these relationships tangible and explorable, we aim to foster a deeper understanding of environmental systems and how they are affected by human activity.

Satellite Imagery

High-resolution imagery from multiple satellites provides the visual foundation for understanding landscape changes.

Environmental Data

Climate metrics, biodiversity indexes, and pollution readings add critical context to visual observations.

Temporal Analysis

Historical imagery sequences allow users to track changes over time and identify acceleration patterns.

TECHNOLOGY

At the core of Google Warming is a custom-built visualization engine that combines multiple data sources and imagery layers. The technical architecture includes:

  • Data processing pipeline for satellite imagery analysis
  • Geospatial mapping infrastructure built on Unreal Engine
  • Real-time data connection to environmental monitoring systems
  • Custom UI for intuitive exploration of complex datasets

IMPACT

Google Warming aims to transform how we perceive and interpret environmental change in digital media. By making visible the often-hidden patterns of resource extraction and ecological damage, the project contributes to critical environmental literacy in the digital age.

The work serves as both an artistic intervention and a practical tool for researchers, educators, and environmental advocates seeking to understand and communicate the scale and character of anthropogenic change.

CHALLENGES

Key challenges in developing this project include:

  • Balancing technical complexity with usability
  • Visualizing abstract environmental data in meaningful ways
  • Navigating the politics of representation
  • Creating systems that work across different scales

FUTURE DEVELOPMENT

Future directions for Google Warming include:

  • Expanded dataset of environmental impact sites
  • Mobile application for field-based exploration
  • Integration with educational curricula
  • Community sourcing of local environmental knowledge
