OVERVIEW

A curated selection of illustration-based projects by Ethan Sheaf-Morrison. These works span editorial commissions, speculative design experiments, and visual research inquiries. Each project navigates its own visual language while contributing to a larger practice concerned with systems, identity, and environmental narratives.

Jump to Approach Jump to Process Jump to Technology

APPROACH

Each illustration is developed through a process of visual exploration, research, and iteration. From pen and ink to digital collage, the selected works reflect a commitment to clarity, criticality, and experimentation across subject matter and technique.

PROCESS

Processes vary from project to project. Some begin with sketching and storyboarding, while others evolve through photographic sourcing or digital compositing. Across all, attention to narrative, symbolism, and composition guides the final outcome.

TOOLS & TECHNIQUES

The works utilise a hybrid workflow of analogue and digital methods. Tools include:

  • Hand-drawn illustration and inking
  • Adobe Photoshop and Illustrator
  • Procreate and iPad sketching
  • Image compositing and texturing

VISUAL LANGUAGE

The projects range in visual tone from bold editorial pieces to softer, more reflective compositions. Each illustration is tailored to its context, drawing on a consistent visual sensibility shaped by clarity, rhythm, and storytelling.

IMPACT

These illustration projects aim to provoke reflection, prompt dialogue, and provide visual clarity on complex ideas. Through their form and content, they strive to make abstract systems more accessible and emotionally resonant.
