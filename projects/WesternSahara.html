/ PROJECT

New Zealand for Western Sahara

/ DATE

2025

/ ROLE

Research, Design, Development

/ TYPE

Research, Data Visualization, Mapping, Web Design

/ OVERVIEW

This project investigates the complex relationship between Aotearoa New Zealand and Western Sahara through the lens of phosphate mining and trade. It explores how the global phosphate supply chain connects these seemingly distant regions and impacts indigenous communities.

/ APPROACH

The research examines the historical and contemporary implications of phosphate mining in Western Sahara, a territory under Moroccan occupation, and its connection to New Zealand's agricultural industry. Through data visualization and mapping, the project reveals the hidden costs of phosphate extraction and its impact on indigenous Sahrawi communities.

/ TOOLS

  • Geospatial data analysis
  • Data visualization
  • Web development
  • Historical research

Key Findings

  • Mapping of phosphate trade routes between Western Sahara and New Zealand
  • Analysis of the environmental and social impacts of phosphate mining
  • Documentation of indigenous resistance and advocacy efforts
  • Visualization of the global phosphate supply chain

Methodology

  • Historical research and documentation
